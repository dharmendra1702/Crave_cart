How the system behaves
Real workflow
Cart accuracy

Cart totals are derived from server-side values to avoid mismatch. Quantity updates stay consistent across pages, and checkout always reflects the latest cart state.

Checkout confidence

Checkout focuses on clarity: delivery address, item list, final amount, and chosen payment mode are displayed together so users can confirm before placing the order.

  1. Discover restaurants and explore menus.

Founder
Profile
Dharmendra Reddy M S
Full-Stack Developer

I created CraveCart to demonstrate a complete food ordering workflow that feels clean and reliable. The focus is on real product behavior: restaurant discovery, menu browsing, cart accuracy, checkout, online payments/COD, and consistent order tracking.

While building CraveCart, my priority was to keep the experience fast for users and predictable for admins. Totals are validated on the server, order statuses follow a stable lifecycle, and email notifications confirm key events so users always know what happened.

This project also reflects how a production-style ordering system should behave: clear UI for decision-making, clean status communication, and controlled operations for maintaining order history.

FAQ
Quick answers
How does COD work?

COD orders are marked PENDING until delivered, then updated to PAID automatically.

Will I get order emails?

Yes. Customer + admin emails are sent after placing the order and when status changes.

Is the site responsive?

Yes. The layout adapts for mobile screens and keeps actions accessible.

Can I contact without an account?

Yes. You can submit the contact form as a guest as well.
